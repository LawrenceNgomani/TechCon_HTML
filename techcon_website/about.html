History

Conferences as structured gatherings trace back to ancient Greece and Rome, where large public forums and religious pilgrimages served as early models of communal discourse. By the 15th century, European trade fairs evolved into proto‐conferences focused on commerce and innovation, setting the stage for modern professional meetings. During the 19th century, industrialization spurred formal congresses like the Congress of Vienna (1815), emphasizing political and technical standardization across nations. The first conference dedicated solely to the World Wide Web, WWW1, took place at CERN from May 25–27, 1994, gathering 380 participants to discuss emerging hypertext standards; it is often called the "Woodstock of the Web". In the 21st century, large-scale tech events such as TED and Nvidia's GTC have expanded the boundaries of tech discourse, broadcasting ideas globally and influencing industry direction.

Mission

TechCon strives to advance the development and ethical application of emerging technologies by fostering open dialogue, cross-disciplinary collaboration, and lifelong learning among professionals. Inspired by organizations like the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM), which since 1947 has promoted professional standards and knowledge sharing in computing, TechCon's mission is to create an inclusive space where attendees can explore innovations ranging from AI and cybersecurity to quantum computing. Additionally, TechCon is committed to preserving tech's historical context—mirroring goals of the Society for the History of Technology (SHOT), founded in 1958 to study technology's societal impacts—by offering sessions that examine how past breakthroughs inform today's challenges.

Notable Past Speakers

Over the years, TechCon has hosted leaders whose insights have shaped technology's trajectory. Below are a few luminaries who have spoken at major tech gatherings, underscoring the caliber of voices TechCon aims to emulate:

Steve Wozniak

As co-founder of Apple in 1976, Steve Wozniak's design of the Apple I and Apple II personal computers sparked the home computing revolution. His talks often emphasize the importance of accessible engineering and grassroots innovation in driving technology forward.

Jimmy Wales

Jimmy Wales founded Wikipedia in 2001 to create a free, collaborative encyclopedia, dramatically changing how knowledge is disseminated and democratizing information worldwide. His presentations typically explore open-source principles and the social impact of collective intelligence.

Jensen Huang

As NVIDIA's CEO since 1993, Jensen Huang has spearheaded the evolution of GPUs from graphics accelerators to fundamental engines for AI research and deployment. His keynote addresses at Nvidia GTC famously unveil cutting-edge AI hardware and visionary roadmaps, inspiring developers and researchers globally.
